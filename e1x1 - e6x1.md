## Ambrosia

The Ambrosia bar fills at a set rate of bar points per second. Each full bar grants Ambrosia equal to luck/100 (fractional luck gives a random chance at an extra one). Bar requirement grows linearly (with offset) relative to total Ambrosia, and doubles twice per order of magnitude at 10^n and 3*10^n for every n starting at 4 - first doubling at 10k, second at 30k, and so on.

Buy the **Patreon bonus** first - it gives a large yamb bar speed buff, typically over +100%. Then buy the **10 tutorial module levels** for +50% cubes and +10% Quarks at a cost of 100 Ambrosia. Both apply regardless of loadout.

Because Ambrosia is freely refundable, maintain multiple specialised loadouts:

| Loadout | When to use | Modules |
|---|---|---|
| Cube | Switch to this before ascending | CM1 only; add LCHM1 and QCHM1 if you have excess Ambrosia |
| Quark | Switch to this after a c15 expo update and during add code usage; open 1 Platonic to activate Quark multipliers | QM1, CQHM1, LQHM1 |
| Luck | Default loadout for most of your time; maximises Ambrosia generation | LM1, CLHM1, QLHM1 |


## Elevator and Lookahead

You have two singularity counts: **current** (top bar, determines penalties) and **highest** (perks page, determines perks and GQ/Exalt shop unlocks).

To advance singularity, you must buy the **AoAG rune** from p4x5 regardless of what else you do. Once bought, you can look ahead 1-5 sings from your current sing depending on which upgrades you own - **aetherflux** (GQ shop) and **discombobulator** (oct shop) each extend lookahead range.

Looking ahead updates current sing (penalties) to the target. Highest sing only ever increases by 1 per lookahead, and only if you were previously at your highest sing. Whenever you buy AoAG, highest sing updates to current sing if that would increase it - meaning some perks unlock after completing a singularity rather than at the start.

With +n lookahead: you can look ahead exactly n by singing normally, exactly 1 by checking "only go up 1 sing," or anywhere between 1 and n by clicking "lock future singularities."

**Example 1:** At s100 with +3 lookahead, jumping to s103 sets current to s103 and highest to s101 (the perks relevant to s101 unlock immediately). Buying AoAG in s103 then sets highest to s103.

**Example 2:** Highest sing is 100, but you descended to s99 with the elevator (still +3 lookahead). Jumping to s102 does not raise highest sing, since you were not at your highest sing when you jumped. Buying AoAG in s102 updates highest sing to 102 and unlocks the relevant perks.

### Mechanics and Tricks

- Export your save before any elevator use.
- You can decrease current sing with the elevator freely - this resets nothing.
- Raising current sing with the elevator (or jumping to the same one) costs a singularity reset.
- To unlock any perk, you only need to beat the singularity below it and look ahead from there.
- Highest sing updates even while inside an Exalt.
- Entering an Exalt taking place in a higher sing, while at your highest sing with AoAG, increases highest sing by +1, as if you'd looked ahead normally.
- Entering or exiting an Exalt does not reset your singularity timer, but grants the GQ you would have earned for the sing you just left.
- Combining these three points lets you push singularities while preserving your timer for upgrades that use it (DELTA and PHI), as long as you have an Exalt available in a higher sing.

## Corruptions and Post-AoAG Gameplay

Post-AoAG corruption setups remain largely stable from p4x2: raise Time Dilation to cap and Hyperchallenge beyond 7, then 9, as you grow stronger - this gains Obtainium for the mythic Wow! talisman and more challenge completions. As challenge completions accumulate to the point of capping your hept setup, begin raising Viscosity beyond 10.

For Octeracts after Cookies 4, `14/14/14/14/14/14/14/14` outperforms `10/14/14/14/14/14/14/14` for a while. Once level 15 corruptions are unlocked, swap immediately to something like `10/15/15/15/15/15/15/15` for octs.

Raising Viscosity to 11 or 12 starts paying off around e2-e3. Each Viscosity level increases difficulty but also increases score significantly.

Overnight only Octeracts and Ambrosia. In this channel, prioritise Octeracts overnight since they give the most immediate benefit. Ambrosia overnighting begins in the next channel.

## New Exalts

Complete the first clear of any Exalt as soon as it unlocks - they range from easy to trivial at unlock.

### e2

Maximum of one completion each from c1-c14 (the Not So Challenging perk bypasses this, adding 5 c8 completions). Use the highest corruption levels you can while still clearing c10. Raise Viscosity last. If you have Cookie 3 and Cx16, turn off auto-platonic upgrades and avoid Omega - Cx16 removes 2x cubes with minimal buff.

### e3

Ascension count multiplier is locked to 1, with a limited ascension budget before debuffs begin (ascension speed halved beyond maximum). You do not gain ascension count from ascending without c10 - turn off auto challenge and manually complete c10. c15 can be run without c10 to gain exponent without spending an ascension. Export saves frequently to correct mistakes.

### e4

Same as e1 but Octeract upgrades are disabled. Slightly harder at A0 in later completions, otherwise similar.

### e5

Same as e1 and e4 but easier; Ambrosia loadouts are disabled. Complete e5x1 as soon as you reach s166 to unlock new upgrades and new Ambrosia modules.


## The s150 Push

Progressing past s150 is tough - there is a double difficulty spike, one at s150 and another at s151. If you land exactly at s150, the best approach is staying at +4 singularities per jump, since the next 2 milestone goals are both multiples of 4.

Ascension speed drops significantly below x1 at this stage, meaning it takes longer to reach 10s ascension time. The time multiplier before 10s ascension time is squared, and since the multiplier itself is below 1, resetting at 1s is roughly 100x worse than resetting at 10s. This causes a large penalty to cubes/sec.

Once you reach **s162** you can purchase **One Mind**, which locks ascension speed to x10 but multiplies cubes based on the difference - you will no longer drop under 10s ascension time, giving a strong early-singularity cube boost.

After One Mind, push one more singularity to reach **s166**, which unlocks Exalt 5. This should take under an hour and the rewards are worth it. After e5x1, move to the next channel and stop pushing singularities to farm resources instead.

If the climb from s100 to s150 was easy, the s150-s166 push should clear in under an hour.

The Octeract upgrade **Infinite Transcription of the One Mind** has no effect until you purchase One Mind in the GQ shop.


## Half-Mind and One-Mind

**Half-Mind** unlocks at s150. It locks the anthill timer to 10x, making ant gains scale linearly rather than quadratically to time when the anthill timer is below roughly 2 seconds. This reduces the global speed debuff impact at higher singularities.

**One-Mind** unlocks around s159-s162+. Without it, you cannot enter c15 challenges, since the game requires 2 seconds of in-game ascension time to enter c15. One-Mind locks ascension speed to 10x and converts multiples of it into cube multipliers. Effects: c15 becomes accessible without waiting, cube/s becomes predictable, and Octeract gains increase (ascension speed affects Oct gain, raised to the 0.5, further increased by some Oct upgrades).

## FAQ

<details>
<summary>What is the difference between current and highest singularity?</summary>

Current sing determines penalties. Highest sing determines perks and GQ/Exalt shop unlocks. They can differ when using the elevator or lookahead.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I lose highest singularity progress by using the elevator?</summary>

No. Highest sing can only increase, never decrease. Descending with the elevator only changes current sing.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why can't I enter c15?</summary>

Between s159 and s162+, entering c15 requires 2 seconds of in-game ascension time. One-Mind fixes this by locking ascension speed to 10x.

</details>

<details>
<summary>When should I raise Viscosity above 10?</summary>

Around e2-e3, once you are capping challenge completions in your hept setup. Each Viscosity level is harder but gives significantly more score.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Should I overnight Ambrosia or Octeracts?</summary>

Octeracts in this channel. Ambrosia overnighting starts in the next channel.

</details>

<details>
<summary>I'm struggling after Singularity 150, should I stop to farm Quarks before progressing?</summary>

Not yet. If you landed exactly at s150, stay at +4 singularities per jump, since the next 2 milestone goals are multiples of 4. Once you reach s162 you can buy One Mind to fix the ascension speed penalty, then push one more to s166 for Exalt 5. After that, move to the next channel and farm resources instead of pushing further.

</details>

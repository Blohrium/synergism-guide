## Content

### s256

After completing s256 (or moving to s256+ after completing s255), a large set of GQ upgrades unlocks. These upgrades provide significant bonuses to ambrosia generation and ambrosia luck. You likely cannot max them immediately.

e7 unlocks after s256. This channel is defined by long exalt completions and large shop upgrade rewards.

### e7

e7 combines e1, e4, and e5, with one key difference: **lifetime octeract and ambrosia effects still apply**. Without GQ/Oct/Amb upgrades there is very little to change e7's speed. After accumulating upgrades at s256, complete e7x10 first — it grants a quark upgrade that reduces wait time for Platonic DELTA and PHI.

e7 runs have no special mechanics beyond possibly struggling in A0. Use an ambrosia loadout with Exalt Singularity Reducer if affordable; otherwise ambrosia loadouts are not critical.

### e3x25

Do not prioritise this until you can finish it within half a day — the reward is weak. Complete e1x30 first if possible; if you can finish e1x30 you should be able to finish e3x25.

Before attempting e1x30, you should be pushing exalts to roughly 29/23/24/10/18/15/10 with ease. Requirements: max artisan, most or all red vouchers from Ramb upgrades, and ~6.5 million ambs or more.

Expected stats for a 1–2 day completion: ~5.5–6 million ambs, 5–10 red vouchers with ~50–70k rambs, and close to e100 octs.

### Amb Production vs Cube Production

When deciding whether to put red ambs into production (luck converter, red sands, dice) vs cube production (artisans, vouchers), model e1x30 as the target. Treat cube production upgrades as "required" for e1x30 — for example, 20 artisans and 10 vouchers costs 27,500 rambs.

At ~1 million ambs (~+34.8% bb speed at max upgrade), the cost-effectiveness frontier for 20 artisans/10 vouchers is ~79k for yamb and ~170k for ramb. Push ramb CEF to ~100k before committing to cube production upgrades.

Beyond this point, e7x20 and e5x20 upgrades give only cube buffs, which transfer to ambrosia only indirectly.

### e7x20

Complete e7x20 as soon as you have the e1x30 upgrade — it is a shop upgrade and is not affected by e7's debuff. The upgrade multiplies cubes by ~20–100× by giving a massive bonus to free levels in GQ upgrades. Waiting for more ambs helps, but the difference is small unless you have 1–2 million ambs.

### e6x25 and Tesseract Stats Check

e6x25 is a major stats wall. Strategy for tight e6 timers is covered in the next channel. Before attempting, use the Tesseract stats check:

Enter e3x26 or e6x25 without auto ants or challenge. Wait for all research to purchase. Check Tesseract per second shown at the top of the screen.

- Minimum to attempt: **5e13 T/s**
- Comfortable: **2e14–5e14 T/s**

Note: during a bell, Cx8 gives 1.25× more cubes, but this does not appear in the A0 tess stats.

Minimum stats: 12.5 million ambs (exalt reducer swap required), and ~1e25 GQ or more if ambs are lower.

## FAQ

```
Q: What does e7 actually do differently from e1?
A: Lifetime octeract and ambrosia effects still apply in e7, unlike e1/e4/e5 which strip those. Otherwise the exalt functions similarly.
```

```
Q: Should I do e3x25 before e1x30?
A: Only if you can finish it within half a day. The reward is weak, and e1x30 should take priority.
```

```
Q: How do I know if I'm ready for e6x25?
A: Use the Tesseract stats check — enter e3x26 or e6x25 without auto ants or challenge, wait for research to buy, and read T/s from the top bar. Aim for at least 5e13 T/s before attempting.
```

```
Q: Do I need Omega in e2x25?
A: No. Do not buy Omega — Cx16 costs 2× cubes with minimal compensation.
```

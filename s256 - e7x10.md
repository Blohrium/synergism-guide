## s256

After completing s256 (or moving to s256+ after completing s255), a large set of GQ upgrades unlocks. These provide significant bonuses to Ambrosia generation and Ambrosia luck. You likely cannot max them immediately.

e7 unlocks after s256. This channel is defined by long Exalt completions and large shop upgrade rewards.


## Singularity Reducer Switcheroo

The 2nd level of Singularity Reducer is expensive and does not break even on cube gain for a long time. The 1st level is essentially free at this stage, so you gain more cubes overall using 1 reducer rather than 2 - the extra cubes from roughly 10m ambs outweigh the smaller cube gain penalty reduction from a 2nd reducer.

There is still a way to benefit from the reduced Hepteract crafting costs that 2 reducers give, without giving up the cube advantage of 1 reducer:

1. Switch to a 1 Singularity Reducer build.
2. Turn off auto-crafting in the Hepteract Forge.
3. Ascend for cubes.
4. Switch to a 2 Singularity Reducer build.
5. Turn on auto-crafting in the Hepteract Forge.
6. Spam ascend a few times to auto-craft your Hepts.

This same process applies to 1 & 2 Exalt Reducers, 0 & 1 Singularity Exalt Reducers, and technically to Cube & Plat upgrades as well.


## e7

e7 combines e1, e4, and e5, with one key difference: **lifetime Octeract and Ambrosia effects still apply**. Without GQ/Oct/Amb upgrades there is very little to change e7's speed.

After accumulating upgrades at s256, complete **e7x10** first - it grants a Quark upgrade that reduces wait time for Platonic DELTA and PHI.

e7 runs have no special mechanics beyond possibly struggling in A0. Use an Ambrosia loadout with Exalt Singularity Reducer if affordable; otherwise Ambrosia loadouts are not critical here.

### Ambrosia Production vs Cube Production

When deciding whether to put red ambs into production (luck converter, red sands, dice) vs cube production (artisans, vouchers), model e1x30 as the target. Treat cube production upgrades as "required" for e1x30 - for example, 20 artisans and 10 vouchers costs 27,500 rambs.

At roughly 1 million ambs (about +34.8% bb speed at max upgrade), the cost-effectiveness frontier for 20 artisans/10 vouchers is roughly 79k for yamb and 170k for ramb. Push ramb CEF to roughly 100k before committing to cube production upgrades.

Beyond this point, e7x20 and e5x20 upgrades give only cube buffs, which transfer to Ambrosia only indirectly.


## e7x20

Complete e7x20 as soon as you have the e1x30 upgrade - it is a shop upgrade and is not affected by e7's debuff. The upgrade multiplies cubes by roughly 20-100x by giving a massive bonus to free levels in GQ upgrades. Waiting for more ambs helps, but the difference is small unless you have 1-2 million ambs.

## e6x25 and Tesseract Stats Check

e6x25 is a major stats wall. Strategy for tight e6 timers is covered in the next channel.

Before attempting, use the Tesseract stats check: enter e3x26 or e6x25 without auto ants or challenge, wait for all research to purchase, and check Tesseract per second shown at the top of the screen.

| Threshold | T/s |
|---|---|
| Minimum to attempt | 5e13 |
| Comfortable | 2e14-5e14 |

During a bell, Cx8 gives 1.25x more cubes, but this does not appear in the A0 Tess stats.

Minimum stats: 12.5 million ambs (exalt reducer swap required), and roughly 1e25 GQ or more if ambs are lower.

## FAQ

<details>
<summary>What does e7 actually do differently from e1?</summary>

Lifetime Octeract and Ambrosia effects still apply in e7, unlike e1/e4/e5 which strip those. Otherwise the Exalt functions similarly.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Should I do e3x25 before e1x30?</summary>

Only if you can finish it within half a day. The reward is weak, and e1x30 should take priority.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I know if I'm ready for e6x25?</summary>

Use the Tesseract stats check: enter e3x26 or e6x25 without auto ants or challenge, wait for research to buy, and read T/s from the top bar. Aim for at least 5e13 T/s before attempting.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need Omega in e2x25?</summary>

No. Do not buy Omega - Cx16 costs 2x cubes with minimal compensation.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How does the Singularity Reducer switcheroo work?</summary>

Switch to 1 reducer and ascend for cubes with auto-crafting off, then switch to 2 reducers, turn auto-crafting on, and spam ascend to craft Hepts cheaply. This gets the cube advantage of 1 reducer and the crafting cost advantage of 2.

</details>

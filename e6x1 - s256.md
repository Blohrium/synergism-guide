## Red Ambrosia and New Ambrosia Modules

After e5x1 you unlock red Ambrosia. Red bar points per second is derived from blue bar points per second, raised to the power of 0.5 above 1,000. The red bar requirement caps at 1 million and stops increasing past that point (it may decrease due to e6 effects).

Red Ambrosia upgrades are roughly sorted by cost. Early on, prioritise upgrades that buff Ambrosia speed/luck or red Ambrosia speed/luck. Luck converter upgrades are expensive but strong production boosts. Cash in red Ambrosia into the two cube-boosting upgrades when pushing singularity or exalt milestones.

Many new Ambrosia modules unlock after e5x1 - see [Abbreviations & Hotkeys](Abbreviations%20&%20Hotkeys.md) for the module list. Use the Ambrosia Heater from the Re-Oct sheet to optimise loadouts.

## Singularity Reducer Tech

If your cube loadout does not have maximum Singularity Reducer (SR), you can benefit from both cost reduction and cube buff simultaneously:

1. Disable auto hept (or auto plats, depending on stage).
2. Ascend with the cube loadout to collect cubes.
3. Swap to the max SR loadout.
4. Re-enable auto hept and ascend.


## GQ Distribution

The two primary cube GQ purchases at this stage are **Citadel of Singularity** and **Cube Flame**. The optimal ratio is 1:2 flame-to-citadel, but only after buying 2 Citadel levels - before that, let the free level exponent from Octeracts guide spending. Spend between 1/3 and 2/3 of flame spending on Absinthe; the optimal flame-to-Absinthe ratio is 3:4, but this is rarely worth reaching.


## DELTAvator

DELTAvator lets you reach a new singularity (unlocking perks and GQ purchases) without resetting the singularity timer for Plat DELTA (up to 10x 3D-8D cubes) and PHI (up to 50 free daily upgrades).

To reach singularity X: unlock singularity in s(X-1), then enter and leave an Exalt (commonly e2) that takes place in at least sing X. You can safely use the elevator down (and back) afterward.

**Example:** e2x19 takes place in s208. Finishing s199, then entering and exiting e2 at 18/25 completions, lands you at s200 with all s200 perks unlocked.

### Caveats

- Only one singularity can be pushed at a time.
- Lookaheads do not work with DELTAvator.
- You must actually complete e2 to progress past s175, and every 11 sings after that. Expect roughly 3x longer runs compared to a normal sing.
- The same approach allows refarming a sing (pseudosinging) and skipping sings if e2 can be finished ahead of highest singularity.


## Perks and Automation

s150 unlocks **auto cube upgrades**. s200 unlocks **auto platonic cube upgrades**. Both can be accessed early inside Exalts, unless the Exalt is at or above your highest singularity, where automation logic becomes unreliable.

From s200 to s205, **Even More Quarks** gives +5% Quarks per singularity. From s205 to s215, +5% every other singularity. The **skrauq** perk unlocking at s200 is also very large. Both Even More Quarks and skrauq scale with **current** singularity, which makes singularity pushing heavily favourable from s200 through at least s220.


## Infinite Upgrades and Vouchers

The 4th GQ shop upgrade unlocks several multiplicative 1,000-level-cap upgrades. Prioritise cubes and ascension speed in roughly a 2:1 cost ratio or 4:3 level ratio for 3D-7D cube gain. Begin buying Obt/Off infinite upgrades only when A0 becomes a bottleneck.

Vouchers unlock from a GQ upgrade or an Octeract upgrade. Each voucher is equivalent to 1 level of all four upgrade types (ascension speed, cubes, Obt, Off), excluding the Ambrosia upgrades and the Qhept upgrade. A voucher shop upgrade is available in e3x20.

A second Qhept unlocks in this phase.

### Octeract Metagenesis

Unlocks at s200 (level 1) and s205 (level 2). Grants free levels of Octeract Cogenesis and Trigenesis via daily code. The game takes the maximum of the listed formula and 1% of the bought level as free levels - free levels can be granted even without existing free levels.


## Exalt Climbing and Ambrosia Bottlenecking

Only attempt Exalts when A0 can be finished in under 3 minutes. Use correct Ambrosia loadouts, ant sac at max reborn elo, and Exalt-specific strategies.

Most shop upgrades from Exalt milestones in this channel are minor, with two exceptions:

**e1x20** - reduces effective singularity by 1 per level (4-level cap). At level 4 in s225, penalties match s221. Worth spending a day or more to complete.

**e3x20** - grants 0.005 vouchers per Exalt completion per level, rounded down (100-level cap). Reasonable to go slightly out of your way for this.

A progression bottleneck begins around s230+. Post-AoAG runs overnighted for Ambrosia and red Ambrosia become necessary. From this point, GQ, Quarks, and Octs should be invested into Ambrosia production. Prioritise Ambrosia speed over luck at roughly a 4:1 to 3:1 level ratio.

Upgrade order: Multi 1 -> Accel 1 -> Multi 2 -> Oct 1 -> Accel 2 -> Multi 3 -> Accel 3 -> Multi 4 -> Accel 4+5 -> Oct 2.

(Multi is from e2x20; Accelerator is from e5x15; Oct amb-luck is from e4x10.)


## e6

e6 unlocks at s216. The goal is to finish a singularity and buy AoAG within the timer. Overrunning by up to roughly 1 minute still yields some cubes. The timer decreases by 20 seconds per completion until e6x25, then by 5 seconds after that. The Obt/Off/cube debuff after the timer increases slightly with each completion.

Complete as many e6 runs as possible whenever you climb singularity or finish other Exalts. Detailed strategies for tight timers are covered in the next channel.

## FAQ

<details>
<summary>What is the DELTAvator and why would I use it?</summary>

It lets you unlock a new singularity's perks and GQ purchases without resetting your singularity timer, preserving DELTA and PHI progress. Use it to push highest sing while staying in a productive run.

</details>

<details>
<summary>When should I attempt Exalts?</summary>

Only when A0 can be cleared in under 3 minutes. Attempting with marginal stats wastes time relative to sitting in post-AoAG for more Ambrosia.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why does progression slow down around s230?</summary>

Ambrosia becomes the primary bottleneck. Post-AoAG runs must be overnighted to accumulate enough Ambrosia and red Ambrosia to push further.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Should I prioritise Ambrosia speed or luck?</summary>

Speed. A rough 4:1 to 3:1 speed-to-luck level ratio is correct at this stage.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What does e1x20 do?</summary>

Reduces effective singularity penalties by 1 per level (4-level cap). At level 4, s225 behaves like s221 for penalties.

</details>

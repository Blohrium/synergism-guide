## Content

Do challenges when they are easy or quick. They can also be timed after a reset — check for easy completions after a transcend before starting a normal transcend run.

**Retry Challenges** does not mean attempting a failed challenge again — if a challenge fails, the right move is to gain more stats, not retry. Its primary use is to automatically re-enter the challenge after completing it, enabling continuous farming. This is significantly improved by **Instant Challenges** from the Quark Shop.

### Challenge Progression Targets

| Stage | Challenge Completions |
|---|---|
| Early reincarnation | 1/0/0/0/0 → 1/0/2/0/0 |
| Mid reincarnation | 2/1/2/0/0 |
| Late reincarnation | 4/3/3/1/1 → 5/3/3/1/1 |
| Pre-transcend | 6/4/3/1/1 → 7/4/4/1/1 |

For corruption-era challenge loadouts, see [Corruption Glossary](Corruption%20Glossary.md).

### Notable Challenges

- **c5** — gives prestige count multiplier; always max for PTR grinding.
- **c7** — gives transcend count multiplier; always max for PTR grinding.
- **c9x1** — unlocks Talismans.
- **c10** — required for ascension count gain in e3; must be exited manually in that exalt to trigger the next ascension challenge.
- **c15** — best challenge 15 exponent (c15 expo) is a key progression stat post-singularity. Run c15 with an Off loadout, ideally with an RCM module.

## FAQ

```
Q: What does Retry Challenges actually do?
A: It re-enters the challenge automatically after you complete it, enabling continuous farming. It is not for retrying a failed challenge.
```

```
Q: When should I do challenges?
A: When they are easy or quick. You can also check for easy completions right after a transcend before starting a normal run.
```

```
Q: Why does c15 matter post-singularity?
A: c15 expo determines quark gain post-AoAG. Running c15 after penalties are removed allows significantly more exponent, which gives more quarks.
```
